  • What is behavioring anyway?

    Yes. You are right. There is no such word called behaviouring in English. It's gramatically incorrect, just like Apple's Think Different.

    Nevertheless behaviouring is a very special word for me. Why? Well. Just think of a computer or any electronic device. It's just a piece of silicon (14Si) packed inside a metal or a plastic. We call it hardware. It simply lies there and doesn't know how to behave. It's creatures like me, the programmers who teach it what things to do and how to do things. Thus adding behaviour(s) to otherwise a piece of silicon.

    And that is called behaviouring. Simple enough?

  • Rinix Mobile Security

    Rinix Mobile Security is a suite of security applications for Android phones. It has anti-virus/ malware scanner, remote mobile tracker & controller, call & sms blocker and privacy advisor. It was a startup project with a dedicated team for UI/ UX, reverse engineering& malware research and app development.

    I headed the app development team and was completely responsible for end to end coding, testing, troubleshooting and release management. I designed the core data structures, malware scan engine and data storage and retrieval logic. The project helped me learn a lot of core computer science concepts especially algorithm design and analysis, system programming, data communication and text processing.

    Initially we started the project as Proof of Concept (PoC) for an improved and reliable malware detection method which is faster than legacy detection methods. When we kicked off we did not have a name for the product. Later when we started development I came up with the suggestion Rinix because it is a very special word. Ri was taken from first two letters of Ritchie in Dennis Ritchie (The inventor of C programming language) and nix was taken from last three letters of Unix (The OS designed by Dennis Ritchie & Ken Thompson). It ended up a good word as it rhymes with Unix and other Unix family of names like Linux, Minix and so on.

    We released Rinix as a free app on June 30, 2013. Thanks to its speed, light-weightness and rich features it gained traction so quickly from users across the world. We reached 1 million count in less than 4 months time! Later we also released a paid version. After around 6 months from the release date Rinix was acquired by Rotella.

  • Speed Booster Pro (Boostify)

    Speed Booster Pro is a simple utility app for Android devices. The app shows the memory and CPU usage of the apps running on the mobile phone. The user can choose to kill the app or select multiple apps which they think is (are) more CPU and memory hungry. Or they can leave the option of automatically killing the resource hungry apps to Speed Booster Pro. The app can also be scheduled to run at a specified interval or run once a day say 6 a.m.

    I designed the UI/ UX of the app and also programmed the entire app. On the way I learned how Linux performs memory management and various commands/ utils relating to Linux memory management.

    The app got generally a very good reception. It got around 100k downloads in 2 weeks time. Later the ownership of the app was transfered to another developer who was willing to acquire it. Currently it has 1 million users.

  • Tamil Nadu Citizen Poll - 2014

    Tamil Nadu Citizen Poll - 2014 is an Android app requested by the Election Commissioner of Tamil Nadu. The app was released to public of Tamil Nadu during 2014 general elections. The app educates the public the Code of Conduct (CoC) to be followed by public and politicians during campaining period and on election day. The app encourages the public to report any event of misconduct directly to the nearest Nodal Officer. Using the app, anyone can take a live picture or video and create an incident. The incident would later be investigated by the Nodal Officer and appropriate action would be initiated against the violater(s) of CoC.

    The app was received well by the public and was used to report 300+ genuine incidents across the state. Recently I heard that the Tamil Nadu Election Commissioner is going to recommend the Election Commission of India to roll out the app for the entire nation in the next general election!

    The app was designed by a team of UI/ UX designers, web developers and app programmers. My responsibility was to co-ordinate with the app programmers and deliver the app code.

  • Lift Prank

    Lift Prank is a simple fun app I'm writing for Android devices. The idea is when you are in a lift, the app will scream different sounds everytime the lift stops at a floor. You can configure the screaming sounds. Most of the times people in a lift would not even see others in the lift. They would just stare at the display which shows the floor number as if they were mummified! After won't it be fun to play a prank around them (mummies)?

    My idea is to listen to the accelerometer readings of the phone and decide if the lift is going up, down or came to halt at a floor. I tried this experiment a couple of times but the accelerometer of my phone is not so accurate. Or may be the algorithm I use to read the accelerometer readings is flawed. I'm still not sure. So I'm looking for a better way to decide the motion of a lift or better algorithm to eliminate the noise that is coming along with the actual accelerometer readings.

    The project is currently stalled. It would be great if someone could help me find a solution for this.

  • Breathe

    Have you ever given a more serious thought about your phone's wall? I mean the home screen where there is a wallpaper. That's the place where you (me too) see more than a 100 times in any given day. Yes there is a wallpaper but who set it? It's you and you set it some 2 weeks ago. And that's it. It's still there, lifeless. Of course there are live wallpapers to add life to it. But the choice of pictures offered by them is limited and they don't know what kind of pictures you like.

    Ok. To understand, consider this scenario. I currently have a wallpaper on my phone which shows the picture of Gautama Buddha in deep meditation with a text below that reads Each morning we are born again. What we do today is what matters the most. Seeing the picture everytime makes me question if I'm spending this moment worth living. Hmmm.. that's a bit of philosophy. Please ignore it! I set that picture long back and after seeing that picture day in and day out for many many days, one day I thought I like pictures of this sort. But my phone is not realizing that. Anyway It has enough time at night when I'm sleeping to think about it. Won't it be much better if it tries to find a similar picture on the internet and set that as the wallpaper and makes me feel happy when I wake up the next morning?

    But my phone can't think on its own, right? So I'm currently teaching my phone how to do that. I thought if I would feel happy if my phone do that for me, a couple others might also feel happy if their phone could do that for them. And so it happened I started writing an Android app and thought Ok. Let's call it Breathe. I'm planning to release Breathe in late September or early November, 2015.
